## Design Context

### Users
Household members (family) using multiple devices — desktops, tablets, phones, possibly TV browsers. Context ranges from lean-back couch browsing to quick "what should we watch" on a phone. The primary job: find something to watch and start streaming it with minimal friction.

### Brand Personality
Modern, slick, effortless. The interface should feel like a premium streaming service — confident and polished without being flashy or overwrought.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Primary reference:** Netflix — both UI patterns and UX flows
- **Anti-reference:** Plex — too cluttered, too many options, poor visual hierarchy
- **Theme:** Dark mode primary, light mode supported
- **Tone:** Cinema-quality presentation of content. The movies are the star; the UI gets out of the way
- **Feel:** Fast, responsive, intuitive. A family member who has never configured anything should be able to browse and play immediately

### Design Principles
1. **Content is king** — Posters, backdrops, and metadata should dominate. Chrome and UI controls should recede until needed.
2. **Zero learning curve** — If someone has used Netflix, they should feel at home instantly. Familiar patterns over clever innovation.
3. **Device-fluid** — Must feel native whether on a phone in bed or a desktop monitor. Not just responsive — adapted.
4. **Quiet confidence** — Premium feel through restraint, spacing, and typography. No gratuitous effects or decorative elements.
5. **Instant feedback** — Every interaction should feel fast and acknowledged. Hover states, transitions, and loading states should all feel snappy.
